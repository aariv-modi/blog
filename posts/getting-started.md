---
title: Getting Started with Writing
date: 2026-05-05
excerpt: Some thoughts on building a regular writing practice and why it's worth the effort.
---

Starting to write regularly is harder than it looks, and easier than it feels.

The hardest part, for me, isn't the writing itself — it's the deciding. Deciding what's worth writing about, deciding it's good enough to publish, deciding to spend the time at all. The actual typing is comparatively painless.

A few things that have helped:

- Lower the bar. Most posts don't need to be definitive. A single observation, clearly stated, is enough.
- Write the way you talk. Editing for clarity later is fine, but starting in a stiff, formal voice rarely produces anything readable.
- Ship often. A post sitting in drafts for two weeks usually gets worse, not better.

If you're thinking about starting your own blog: just start. The first few posts will feel awkward. That's the price of admission.
